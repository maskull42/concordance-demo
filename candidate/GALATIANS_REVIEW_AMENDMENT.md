# Galatians Rule 3 review amendment

The first Galatians author review used response-local position handles correctly at the schema level but incorrectly at the semantic level. The handles `P1` through `P4` were independently shuffled for every response. Codex treated them as globally stable when preparing four recommendations, and A.G. Elrod confirmed those recommendations in good faith.

On 2026-07-14, A.G. Elrod approved an immutable superseding amendment. It changes exactly four response-local handles while preserving the original review and evaluation as historical artifacts:

1. Response 2 changes from `P1` to its local `P2`, “Believers' faith in Christ.”
2. Response 3 changes from `P1` to its local `P2`, “Believers' faith in Christ.”
3. Response 6 changes from `P4` to its local `P2`, “Christ's own faith or faithfulness.”
4. Response 7 changes from `P4` to its local `P3`, “Christ's own faith or faithfulness.”

Responses 1, 4, 5, and 8 remain unchanged. The corrected distribution is four endorsements of believers' faith in Christ and four endorsements of Christ's own faith or faithfulness. Galatians therefore fails Rule 3 because only two positions are represented, where the locked threshold requires three.

The same failure mode was audited across all 64 Rule 2 first-pass mappings. Sixty-three mappings align with their response-local position label, rationale, and quoted evidence. The remaining John Brown/Grok partial fit had already been superseded by the approved `author-review-2` amendment to `null / outside_map`.

The amendment authorizes no provider calls and changes no source, prompt, response, or threshold.
